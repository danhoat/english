>Think vs. Suppose vs. Assume
You'll typically find the word "think" in sentences where someone is giving their opinion on what they believe, or what they believe will happen. Ex. 1) "I think that you look very pretty in that dress" 2) "I think that it will rain tomorrow" While you can find the word "suppose" in sentences where someone is responding to someone else's belief. Ex. Person #1: "Tomorrow's math test is probably going to be very difficult" Person #2: "Yeah, I suppose so" Hope this helps you understand a little better!
    supposing is assuming something; you're thinking about something that could be wrong. I suppose he didn't like me. (I recognize I could be wrong.) I think he didn't like me. (That's my interpretation and still haven't thought a lot about it.)
    "Suppose" = something is assumed - you may or may not agree with it, but you are going along with the idea "Think" = your own thoughts about something, and it does not matter if the other person agrees
    suppose implies some uncertainty. think is similar, but without the implied uncertainty. I think you are correct, leaves unsaid that you might not be correct. I suppose you are correct infers that I know you might not be correct. You have found an English subtlety.
